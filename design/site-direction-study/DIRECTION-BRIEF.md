# BuildKit site redesign — decision brief

## Product job

The homepage should make a serious visitor understand three things quickly:

1. Kruz can take an ambiguous problem from first brief to a working product.
2. The work spans product judgment, interface quality, AI systems, and implementation without feeling scattered.
3. There is enough real, specific proof to justify starting a conversation.

## What the redesign removes

- gradient display text;
- dot-grid and glowing-orb decoration;
- rounded-card walls;
- technology badges as the primary proof;
- placeholder testimonials;
- generic “AI-powered solutions” language without a specific point of view.

## Direction tradeoffs

| Direction | Strongest quality | Risk | Best fit |
|---|---|---|---|
| Field Journal | Distinctive, thoughtful, credible | Less conventionally “tech” | Founder-led studio and complex custom builds |
| Proof Ledger | Clearest evidence and service structure | Parchment surface may feel less immersive | Buyers who need confidence, scope, and process clarity |
| Studio Monograph | Strongest visual product-studio presence | Can feel larger than a one-person practice | Product launches, partnerships, and high-end web work |

## Evaluation questions

- Which direction feels most like the builder Kruz is becoming, not the template he started with?
- Does the first screen make the outcome clearer than the technology?
- Do the three projects feel like evidence rather than a gallery?
- Is the email CTA earned by the time the visitor reaches it?
- Does the mobile version retain hierarchy instead of simply stacking desktop cards?

## Shared accessibility floor

All three systems use a minimum 4.5:1 text contrast target for text that carries information. Decorative hairlines and disabled ornament may be lower. Motion is not required to understand any section and respects reduced-motion preferences.
